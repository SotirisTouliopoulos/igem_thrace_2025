Introduction

Through our social media posts, videos, and collaborative podcasts, we’ve been on a mission: raising awareness for mental health, unveiling the intricacies of our iGEM project, and making synthetic biology accessible to everyone. We’ve broken barriers, sparked curiosity, and shared stories, all in an effort to bridge the gap between science and society.

Podcasts

Μental health month: Our Podcast with SDG Students Hub DUTH

In the spirit of Mental Health Month, our iGEM team had the opportunity to collaborate with the SDG Students Hub DUTH. Together, we delved into a thought-provoking podcast discussion centered around our project's core theme: depression and synthetic biology. This collaboration not only allowed us to explore the fascinating intersections between science and mental health but also aligned with Sustainable Development Goal 3 - "Good Health and Well-being."

During the podcast, we had the privilege of sharing our vision for addressing depression through innovative synthetic biology solutions, raising awareness about this pressing issue, and fostering meaningful conversations about mental health. We also gave some details about synthetic biology and shared some historical facts. To ensure that we are addressing the concerns and questions of our Instagram Followers, we conducted an Instagram Q&A session, and we answered them during the podcast. In addition, we presented the team members and their roles. At the end of the podcast, we shared some fun and interesting facts about our team!

Pancreatic cancer & Depression: Our Podcast with Patras Medicine iGEM

We were honored to be invited as guests on Patras Medicine iGEM podcast series called ‘The SynBio Voice #2’. Our discussion primarily focused on the connection between pancreatic cancer, which was the main theme of their project, and depression. Delving into the depths of these diseases, our conversation explored the intricate link between them.

iGEM is ...

We decided to capture the essence of iGEM through the eyes of iGEM team members. We invited teams from across the globe to complete the phrase, "iGEM is..." and used their responses to create a video that reveals the multifaceted nature of iGEM.

The video was presented at the 1st Panhellenic Conference of Students Bioscientists, fostering discussions between the conference participants. We also shared it on social media. This project was our way of highlighting teamwork, exploration, and the curiosity of the iGEM community.
